Fort Canning Park

Experience

Fort Canning Park, an iconic hilltop landmark has witnessed many of Singaporeâ€™s historical milestones. The hill once sited the palaces of 14th century Malay Kings and served as the Headquarters of the Far East Command Centre and British Army Barracks. The decision to surrender Singapore to the Japanese on 15 February 1942 was also made on the hill, in the Underground Far East Command Centre, commonly known as Battle Box.

What to Look Out!

Today, Fort Canning Park is a venue for celebrations. Its expansive, sprawling lawns play host to concerts, theatre productions and festivals such as Shakespeare in the Park, Ballet Under the Stars, Shakespeare in the Park and Films at the Fort, while weddings, parties and gatherings are a regular sight in the park's venue spaces.

How to Get There

By MRT, alight at Clarke Quay or Dhoby Ghaut, and walk about 10 minutes to get to the park
